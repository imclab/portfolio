Robot programming

Tags: Micro-controllers, low-level programming, electric circuits

This was my first year project at university. We had to build and program a robot to perform a diverse set of tasks. We were given total freedom in our implementation and in the management of our time.

  • No master classes. We relied on the micro-controller's specification.
  • Deep understanding of the hardware. Knowledge of the timing charts, diagrams of the components and control registers.
  • The robot could play music, follow intricate lines, detect nearby objects and escape from mazes, among other things.
  • No operating system. We built a realtime cooperative multitasking scheduler in order to process the data from our various sensors.
  • The robot was built from a kit (similar to Arduino).
  • Winner of the Philip and Lily Malouf prize for passion and excellence.

Started in the winter semester of 2007.

Compiler construction

Tags: JIT compilation, Common Lisp, research papers

In 2007, I started working on a Just-in-Time (JIT) compiler for the open source CLisp Virtual Machine. I started development on my own because I wanted to make CLisp faster. I am now an official member of the CLisp team.

  • CLisp is over 600k LOC. It is one of the most used Lisp compilers.
  • I read several research papers from relevant journals of the IEEE.
  • I learned the various transformations that exist to optimize code.
  • Debugging the dynamically generated code was a real challenge. Especially in the presence of exceptions, tag-bodies, unwinds...
  • I had to generate machine code that balanced low-level concerns like cache pressure, register pressure and branch prediction.
  • As of winter 2008, the compiler is functional and in beta.

Started in the autumn of 2007. Development is ongoing.
